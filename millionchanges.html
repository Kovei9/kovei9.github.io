Million Changes — Mobile App Prototype

A mobile app demo produced at GIMM Works where I was trained into the Art Lead role. I owned the visual direction, reward systems, and much of the game feel while collaborating with 3D artists and developers to build an environment that evolves as the player progresses.

Role: Art Lead Art Direction Concept Art 3D Environment Oversight Unity Mobile Prototype

Overview

Million Changes was the project used to train me into the Art Lead position at GIMM Works. The client needed a mobile app experience focused on forming habits and rewarding progress over time. I had significant ownership over the art style, the way rewards are represented, and how the app communicates growth to the player through visuals and small game-like interactions.

While I was not focused on UI implementation or the animal content, I directed the overall look, cleared animations, and worked closely with a 3D environment artist whose work became one of the core metaphors for player progress.

Responsibilities

As Art Lead on this prototype, I:

  • Defined the overall art direction and tone for the app.
  • Created concept work for badges, rewards, and progression systems.
  • Collaborated with the client to align game functions with their goals and constraints.
  • Guided a 3D artist building an environment that visually upgrades as players level up.
  • Cleared animations and made sure they fit both the style and intended player feeling.

Badges and Reward Concepts

A big part of Million Changes was making progress feel visible and satisfying. I explored different badge and reward systems that could fit within the client’s goals, while staying flexible enough for future scaling as more habits or “changes” were added.

Progressive 3D Environment

One of the most visible rewards in Million Changes is a 3D house and yard that starts out cluttered and run-down, then gradually transforms into a clean, welcoming space as the user levels up. I provided direction and feedback on this environment while my 3D teammate handled modeling and animation.

I reviewed passes of the environment as it evolved, making sure the changes felt meaningful and readable on a mobile screen, and that the “before vs. after” contrast matched the emotional journey we wanted for the player.

Collaboration and Direction

Million Changes was also where I first stepped into balancing client needs, dev constraints, and artist bandwidth. I had to think not just about what looked good on a screen, but what was realistic to build and iterate on in a mobile prototype with limited time.

I spent a lot of time translating between what the client wanted, what the devs needed, and what my artists could deliver. That experience carried forward into later projects where I was fully responsible for art pipelines and quality control.
