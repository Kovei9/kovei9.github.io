About Me

Hello there! I'm Kenzie. I'm a 2D/3D Generalist, and I think my greatest strength is my adaptability.


I started as a 2D animator, until I went to BSU and studied under the GIMM (Games, Interactive Media, Mobile Technologies) program.


Throughout all of this, I was working 2-3 jobs while a full time student. My sophmore year alone, I was bartending at 20, working as a 2D animator for GIMM Works, selling furniture at a consignment store, and balancing everything as a full-time student.

By my senior year, I was definitely exhausted, but my peers and coworkers helped me push through to the end. We built a lot of really great things together, and I can't thank them enough.


During my time in University, I’ve worked for two studios (technically three), gaining about two years of in-industry experience as both a 2D/3D Generalist and an art lead on multiple client-funded projects.

I’ve been told I work best in roles where I can wear multiple hats, moving between 2D, 3D, or even implementing animation trees, VFX, or shader graphs in Unity and Unreal.

I’m very used to jumping between departments and stepping in wherever I’m needed.


I still think my technical skills can improve across any medium, but I don’t see that ever stopping. I’m confident in my ability to learn quickly while I work, and I try to stay around people who push me to be the best artist (and person!) I can be.

I think I’d be a strong fit in an indie environment, but I’m also open to being trained into a more specialized role.

That said, animation is what I enjoy the most. I really love bringing characters to life.


I also genuinely enjoy working with people. While remote work is appealing, I do my best work in environments where I can collaborate and stay connected with a team.
