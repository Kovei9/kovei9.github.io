Kestrel VR

Realistic digital-twin VR simulation used to train wildlife professionals how to band and measure kestrels in Boise, Idaho.

RoleArt Lead • Technical Artist • Rigger
EngineUnity (Hand Tracking VR)
FocusRealism, Precision Interaction, Training
TeamArtists + Developers + Wildlife Researchers

Project Overview

Kestrel VR is a realistic VR training simulation funded by wildlife professionals who monitor and tag kestrels in Idaho. The experience teaches proper handling, measurement, and banding techniques using full hand tracking instead of controllers.

My Art & Technical Contributions

  • Designed and built a highly complex, production-ready kestrel rig
  • Weight painted and optimized deformation for wings, legs, neck, eyelids, and subtle head motion
  • Designed the rig to support multiple simultaneous animations
  • Created animations for blinking, head movement, and handling states
  • Produced UI concept art and the project logo

Art Lead & Quality Control

  • Established a quality control pipeline for models entering Unity
  • Reviewed anatomical accuracy of wildlife tools and assets
  • Translated developer animation needs into actionable tasks for artists
  • Designed a model review system so artists could self-check in Unity
  • Resolved pipeline issues caused by unclear animation and rig requirements

Art Direction Decisions

Before

Assets lacked anatomical accuracy and real-world specificity.

After

Updated proportions, feather density, eye scale, tools, and accuracy to match real kestrel handling.
